# Sanju — Writing Portfolio
 
This is my personal writing portfolio — a small, quiet corner of the internet for the part of me that exists outside of code.
 
I'm an engineer by profession, but writing has been with me a lot longer. It started in the ninth grade with songs and poetry — a way of putting language to things I didn't have another way of saying — and it followed me through college, where that same instinct started winning me poetry competitions I never expected to place in. These days it shows up in unexpected places: the technical documentation and GitHub READMEs I write for work carry the same instinct for clarity and rhythm that my poetry always did.
 
What I actually want to write is thrillers and psychological drama — stories that don't just show an audience a mystery, but hand them evidence and let them build a confident, wrong theory of their own, right up until the story proves them wrong in a way they didn't see coming. I want the reader leaning forward, quietly investigating the story alongside the characters, and I want to be the writer who lets them get just close enough before the ground shifts.
 
This site holds that side of me — my creative statement, and two stage plays ("Case?" and its companion piece, "Inheritance") that I hope say more about what kind of writer I want to become than any bio ever could.
